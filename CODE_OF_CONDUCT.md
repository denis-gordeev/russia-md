# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Russia.md is an open repository for sourced, reviewable work about Russia. Participation in issues, pull requests, discussions, and review should stay factual, respectful, and useful.

## Expected behavior

- Critique claims, sources, and implementation details rather than people.
- Bring evidence when discussing factual disputes.
- State uncertainty directly when a claim is not yet verified.
- Keep reviews actionable and specific.
- Respect contributors working on sensitive historical, political, or social topics.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, harassment, insults, or intimidation.
- Slurs, discriminatory language, or demeaning stereotypes.
- Doxxing or sharing private information without permission.
- Propaganda-style campaigning that is not grounded in verifiable sources.
- Repeated bad-faith argumentation that blocks editorial or technical work.

## Handling contentious topics

This repository covers politically charged and historically disputed material. Higher tension does not lower the bar.

- Sources come before rhetoric.
- Strong claims need strong attribution.
- Disagreement is acceptable; unsupported certainty is not.
- Maintainers may pause or close threads that stop being productive.

## Enforcement

Report conduct issues privately to `denis.gordeev@proton.me`.

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments, pull requests, issues, or contributions that violate this code of conduct. Repeated or severe violations can lead to temporary or permanent exclusion from repository participation.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to repository spaces and to public interactions made in the context of representing Russia.md.
